Midway through 2023 i started working on Andromeda Adventure, an augmented reality game created with the purpose of helping undergrads learn physics and math, of course, they would grade the way you performed in the game, so i dont think it will be that fun for people that will be playing it. When i started working on this project, the first level was already completed, as well as the web platform, so i mainly worked in helping to plan the second level and teaching people at my campus to play the game.

About the game

This game works by using the camera of your phone to detect a marker, then to display the main situation the level is putting you in, then, with controls that would pop up on the screen, you will be able to solve the problem. These problems are usually physics and math related, so the controls consists of number inputing, and ways to look at the values of the problem. To be able to play, you need an account that you create in a website, as every score you get while playing is recorded and then sent to the teacher of the class you are taking. In the web platform, you are able to see your grades, play sessions and assignments.
